About

After getting my Associates degree from Darton College in 1996, I moved to Atlanta to start Southern Polytechnic State University (SPSU). I began taking classes during the day while working nights and weekends pushing carts at the local Sam's Club. After busting my butt for several months in what turned out to be one of Atlanta's worst winters on record, I found an ad for an overnight computer operator job in the financial aid department at SPSU.

I continued my education until I decided I wanted to move out of off-campus housing and got myself into debt by purchasing a new vehicle (worst decision ever). I then decided to take a semester off to catch up with some mounting debts. That one semester ended up being more like 5 years long.

I was always interested in programming, but I couldn't seem to land any entry level programming jobs or internships without a college degree. I ended up trying many different fields and attained a few certifications. I worked in QA, computer repairs, help desk, systems implementations, and network engineering. Even during these different jobs, I still worked on the side learning scripting languages and a little programming where ever I could.

I knew I wanted to go back to finish college and an unfortunate lay-off ended up putting me in a help desk job in downtown Atlanta, right next to Georgia State University. I wanted to go back to SPSU, but the proximity of GSU made it very easy for me to finish my degree quickly, while working full time.

I was working on contract (co-op) at Georgia Pacific when I finished my degree in 2006. Almost ten years after I started my degree, but I was finally done. Luckily for me, the market was starting to recover from the tech bubble and hadn't quite made it to the housing bubble yet, so I started a contract at Blue Linx and that is where my career began.
